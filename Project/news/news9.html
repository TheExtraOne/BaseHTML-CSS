swamp mirror

By Kate Mihasjova | May 13, 2022 | 6 min to read

Keywords: Travelling, School news

This weekend I've crossed out one more point in my wish list. It was dedicated to a very marvelous and mysterious place, that is called Yelnya. I know we should talk about environmental problems such as recycling, global warming, pollution, deforestation and etc., but hold your horses! Give me a chance to share a recent experience with you and after that I’m going back to our topic.

What is swamp? Ok, as I've said it before, I understand an essence of forests, fields, mountains, seas and even desserts, but swamp is still incomprehensible for me. Bog wears a bone-chilling fame, but I want to broadcast some thoughts about a magnificence of this place. This trip was like a breath of fresh air especially when you wanted to renew yourself. I’ve been burst because of feelings! Alien nature smashes your feelings and bends your will. You're like a deaf in this voiceless swamp: the summer is coming, but black water portals without bottom are still freezing and the green is so young.

It is a magic powerful place. Probably, too ancient to be disturbed by nosy humanity. But ones it has been almost totally destroyed by fire. The terrifying fire burnt 80% of swamp's territory in 2002. The only reason was someone’s forgotten cigarette. After that terrible cataclysm hundreds of experts, volunteers, locals and world famous organizations tried to protect Yelnya. So why is Yelnya so special?

1) It is the oldest and biggest swamp in Europe. The age of Yelnya bog is about 9000 years. A central part of the bog is convex and 7 meters higher than the edge, that makes it unique.

2) One square swamp's kilometer produces twice bigger amount of clean oxygen, than the forest does.

3) Peat (a dark brown soil made from decaying plants that you can burn as fuel or that you can put around living plants to help them grow) absorbs CO2 and slows down climate warming because of "greenhouse effect". If people destroy the bog, the peat will release into the atmosphere tons of that gase.

4) During spring and autumn migrations more than 5000 cranes and 10000 geese depend on Yelnya for rest and feeding. This is also a home for red book species of plants and animals, huge amount of which you can observe only here. Scientists here discovered white partridge, that had been considered extinct before.

5) Yelnya purifies water through moss and peat. They work as a natural filter, making the water harmless: vast stretches of moss produce a humic acid, that destroys bacterial hazard.

Yelnya without lying is Europe's lungs, a home for million creatures and a huge water reservation, that is why we should keep an eye on it and, of course, it is worth visiting.
